# PaperWise Patterns

Use this file only to calibrate the **product feel** of the page.

It is not a source of implementation code.  
It is a reference for what kind of reading experience to imitate.

## What PaperWise suggests

The public PaperWise products point toward a **reader-first** experience:

- the document stays central
- AI explanation stays grounded to the document
- the page behaves like a reading workspace, not a marketing page
- the reader can move between source and explanation quickly

Relevant public references:

- [PaperWise Site](https://www.paperwise.site/)
- [paperwise.dev](https://paperwise.dev/)

## What to absorb

Absorb these patterns:

- side-by-side reading
- grounded explanation tied to the source
- quick access to figure and formula context
- persistent sense of where the reader is in the paper
- support for asking both local and global questions

## What not to absorb

Do not copy a generic chat-first product frame.  
Do not replace the paper with a summary card stack.  
Do not drift into conference-promo layout or landing-page aesthetics.  
Do not let AI commentary float free from the source paragraph, figure, or formula.

## Boundary

This skill is **inspired by** PaperWise-like reading workflows.  
It is **not** a clone of a proprietary product, and it should not pretend that PaperWise is a public Claude skill.
